Overview Of My Trading

I have a strong passion for the financial markets. I trade and invest in multiple assets classes, "listed below". Over the years I've developed an approach to the markets that has allowed me to realize consistent returns both long and short term.

Assets I Trade

  • US Equities

    Occasionally I will add equates to my portfolio by ether trading in small-medium cap stocks or investing longer-term in large-cap stocks.

  • US Options

    I trade options on US equities and ETF's. all options positions are multi-leg, risk defined strategies which on average 20 - 40 to expiration.

  • FOREX

    The foreign exchange market is where I have most of my experience. I Trade Forex interday, "Scalping" and short-term positioning.

  • Bitcoin

    Bitcoin and cryptocurrencies is a market that I just recently got involved in. Currently, I'm only making long-term investments in Bitcoin.

Technical Analysis

I use Technicals as my primary form of analysis, which allows me to determine the future course of the price of an asset, or the market as a whole. Technical Analysis also provides a way to time entries, helps in identifying risk and finding price levels to target.

Economic & Fundamental

Geopolitics, Macroeconomics, Central Banks, Monetary Policies and company fundamentals. These are important to track and helps in identifying market risks. I do not trade solely off these type of analysis, rather together with the technicals can form highly probable trade ideas.

Types Of Technicals I Use

  • BreakOuts

    I use breakouts of price to identify a resumption of trend or a passable trend reversal.

  • CandleStick Patterns

    Candle Patterns are good indications of price finding a level of support or being resisted from going higher.

  • Chart Patterns

    These Patterns form when price consolidates. I identify these areas and am able to anticipate and time a breakout.

  • Elliott Wave

    I use Elliott wave on hourly charts and up to help identify where price lies within a trend and its future direction.

  • Fibonacci

    Fibonacci retracements and expansion, I use to spot levels of support and resistance.

  • Reversals

    A reversal pattern forms when price consolidates, I utilize these patterns to spot trend reversals.

  • Seasonality

    Also called Time Series Analysis. A good way to find a period in time where price will likely rise or fall.

Stratagy Overview

After forming a future outlook of an asset through my analysis. I then look to deploy a suitable strategy in line with my expectations and tailored around the prevailing market conditions.

Forex Strategies

With options, I focus more on building a portfolio with trades lasting 20-30 days. The Forex strategies I implement have shorter time horizons, such as interday - "scalping" strategies to short term strategies lasting no longer than a week.

Options Strategies

  • Vertical Spreads

    A diverse strategy that I implement when the price of an asset has a clear directional trend. These are my most used strategies.

  • Butterfly Spreads

    I use this strategy when the price of an assert is consolidating or trading in a defined range and volatility is high.

  • Broken Wing Spreads

    A strategy that I implement to reduce risk and maximize return by adding on to an existing vertical spread forming an Iron Condor.

  • Iron Condor

    A strategy that I implement to reduce risk and maximize return by adding on to an existing vertical spread forming an Iron Condor.

  • Calendar Spreads

    I look to execute this strategy when the price is consolidating and expecting a large spick in volatility.

Risk Management Overview

The fundamental key to any trade or investment is understanding and managing the risks associated with it. I manage risk through diversification in uncorrelated assets and by staying disciplined in following a rigorous set of risk management rules.

  • Managing Options Risk

    I focus on maintaining a slightly directional to market neutral portfolio. all positions are risk defined and are closed when hitting around 50% of max profit.

  • Managing Forex Risk

    The risk is always defined, no greater than 1% of available account equity per trade. 2 to 1 reward/risk ratio, utilizing multiple targets and scaling into positions.
